# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload
- Facilitator: Usually Project Manager, but can rotate
- Attendees: Full delivery team (Developers, QA/Testers, UX/UI Designer, DevOps Engineer)
- Optional: Product Manager, Business Analyst, QA Lead (depending on sprint focus)

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync
- Role-specific improvement areas:
  - **Developers**: Code quality, development workflow, tooling
  - **QA/Testers & QA Lead**: Test coverage, automation, bug detection
  - **DevOps Engineer**: CI/CD efficiency, deployment reliability, monitoring
  - **UX/UI Designer**: Design handoff process, design system maturity
  - **Business Analyst**: Requirement clarity, stakeholder engagement
  - **Project Manager**: Communication effectiveness, meeting efficiency
